Nézobly (also spelled Bazobli) is a town in the far west of Ivory Coast, near the border with Liberia. It is a sub-prefecture of Toulépleu Department in Cavally Region, Montagnes District. The town is approximately three kilometres from the border, but there is no official border crossing in the vicinity. Nézobly was a commune until March 2012, when it became one of 1,126 communes nationwide that were abolished.

[2] In 2014, the population of the sub-prefecture of Nézobly was 6,679. [3] The eight villages of the sub-prefecture of Nézobly and their population in 2014 are:[3] - Kahibli (637) - Klaon (906) - Kpably (662) - Nézobly (1,911) - Tiabolébly 1 (523) - Touaplébli (704) - Toyébli (1,072) - Zaïgbopleu (264) - "Côte d'Ivoire".
