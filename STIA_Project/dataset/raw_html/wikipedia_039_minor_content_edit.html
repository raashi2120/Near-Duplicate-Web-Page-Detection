Hayley Rebecca Simmonds (born 22 July 1988) is a British racing cyclist, who currently rides for UCI Women's Continental Team DAS–Hutchinson.[5]

Career

[edit]

Simmonds attended King's School, Worcester from 1999 until 2006.[6] She studied Natural Sciences at Newnham College, Cambridge, and completed a doctorate in Experimental Chemistry at Gonville and Caius College, Cambridge in 2016. She was originally a rower at Cambridge, taking component in the 2009 reserve crew Boat Race, in advance of switching to cycling in 2011.[7][8]

In 2015, she won the British National Time Trial Championships,[9] and in August 2015 she set a recent British national 10 mile time record with a time of 19:46.[10] Simmonds joined the UnitedHealthcare team in January 2016, yet in June of that year she announced that she was leaving the team in the wake of struggling to locate an efficient position on their Wilier Triestina time trial bicycles.[11]

In November 2020, Simmonds signed a contract with CAMS–Tifosi, for the 2021 season.[12]
